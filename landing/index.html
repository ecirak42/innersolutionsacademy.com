The Panic Attack Loop

A panic attack is a feedback loop of adrenaline. When you fear the sensations, your body treats those sensations as a threat and releases more adrenaline. The more you resist, the worse it gets.

Your instinct might be to fight and control the sensations of panic. But fighting the sensations reinforces that they are dangerous, so your nervous system keeps sounding the alarm.
